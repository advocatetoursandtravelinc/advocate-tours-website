The Province Next Door

Rizal Province has long been the favorite escape of Manila residents — and for good reason. Wrapping around the eastern edge of Metro Manila along the foothills of the Sierra Madre mountain range, Rizal offers a striking contrast to the urban sprawl just beyond its borders: cool mountain air, forested ridgelines, cascading waterfalls, and a creative community of artists and craftspeople who have made this highland province their home. For international travelers based in Manila or arriving through NAIA, Rizal is an ideal first or last stop — close enough for a half-day, rich enough for a full weekend.

Masungi Georeserve

The single most remarkable attraction in Rizal — and one of the most extraordinary conservation projects in Southeast Asia — is the Masungi Georeserve. Set within the Upper Marikina River Basin Protected Landscape in Baras, Masungi is a private reforestation project that has transformed a once-degraded limestone landscape into a thriving forest georeserve, now protecting over 2,700 hectares of critical watershed and biodiversity.

Visitors explore the reserve along guided trails strung with hand-crafted structures built from salvaged materials: suspension bridges of rope and bamboo, a spider-web-like net platform stretched between limestone outcrops, spiral staircases descending into rocky gorges, and wooden lookouts perched above the forest canopy. The entire experience is designed not just as an adventure but as an immersion in conservation — guides explain the work of replanting, the wildlife returning to the area, and the legal battles fought to protect the land from quarrying and illegal settlers. Masungi is booked weeks in advance; reservations are essential.

Daranak & Batlag Falls

In the town of Tanay, the Daranak Falls tumble into a wide natural swimming pool surrounded by forest — a refreshing destination popular with families and groups looking to cool off after a morning hike. The water is clear and cool, the setting lush and peaceful. A short trail leads upstream to the smaller but equally lovely Batlag Falls, where fewer visitors venture and the forest feels more intimate. Both falls are easily combined into a single afternoon outing.

Calinawan Cave

Also in Tanay, Calinawan Cave is a system of interconnected limestone chambers accessible by boat and on foot. The cave's main chamber is cathedral-like in scale, with shafts of light piercing the darkness and thousands of swiftlets nesting in the upper reaches. Local guides lead visitors through the cave's passages, pointing out unusual rock formations and the colony of cave-dwelling creatures that have made this subterranean world their home. The experience is earthy, physical, and deeply atmospheric.

Antipolo — Art, Faith & Views

Antipolo City, the provincial capital, has evolved into one of the Manila region's most vibrant creative hubs. The Pinto Art Museum — a sprawling complex of Mediterranean-style gallery buildings set in terraced gardens — houses an impressive collection of contemporary Filipino art and is one of the country's finest art institutions. Nearby, the Our Lady of Peace and Good Voyage Shrine draws hundreds of thousands of pilgrims each year, and the ridge above Antipolo offers sweeping views of Metro Manila's skyline glittering below — particularly spectacular at night.

Mt. Daraitan & the Tinipak River

For those with more energy, the overnight trek to Mt. Daraitan in Tanay is one of the most rewarding hikes near Manila. The trail passes through the Tinipak River — a stunning gorge of white limestone boulders and impossibly clear water — before ascending through forest to a summit with views stretching from the Sierra Madre to Manila Bay. The peak stands 742 meters above sea level, and the combination of river trekking and summit hiking makes it a favorite of experienced day-trippers and weekend adventurers alike.

Practical Tips

Rizal Province is best explored by private car or hired van. Masungi Georeserve requires advance online booking and runs in limited guided groups — reserve at least two to three weeks ahead. Daranak Falls, Calinawan Cave, and Antipolo can all be done as independent half-day trips from Manila. For the best experience, combine Masungi with a Tanay waterfall visit on the same day. Avoid weekends if possible, as Rizal's proximity to Manila makes popular spots crowded.
