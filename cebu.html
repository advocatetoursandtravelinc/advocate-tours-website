The Heart of the Visayas

Cebu is the Philippines' oldest city and its second most important urban center — but it wears its history lightly, with a confidence that comes from being both an ancient trading port and a thoroughly modern destination. The island of Cebu stretches 225 kilometers from north to south, and within that span you'll find everything from colonial churches and bustling night markets to pristine dive sites, towering waterfalls, and one of the most extraordinary wildlife encounters on earth: swimming alongside the world's largest fish.

Cebu City — History at Every Corner

Begin in Cebu City, where history is woven into the everyday. Magellan's Cross — planted by Ferdinand Magellan in 1521 to mark the first mass baptism in the Philippines — stands in a small chapel on the corner of the city's oldest street, still venerated by locals who light candles and pray beneath it daily. A short walk away, the Basilica Minore del Santo Niño houses the country's oldest religious relic, the Santo Niño de Cebú, a small statue of the Child Jesus brought by Magellan as a gift. The basilica draws thousands of pilgrims every day and becomes the center of the universe every January during the Sinulog Festival, the Philippines' grandest street celebration.

Fort San Pedro, a triangular Spanish colonial fort built in 1738, overlooks the harbor and offers a glimpse of Cebu's strategic importance through the centuries. The nearby Carbon Market, the oldest public market in the Philippines, assaults the senses wonderfully with its mountains of tropical fruit, dried fish, woven baskets, and the animated bargaining of thousands of shoppers.

Swimming with Whale Sharks in Oslob

The single most talked-about experience in Cebu — and perhaps in all of the Philippines — is swimming alongside whale sharks in the small coastal town of Oslob, about two and a half hours south of the city. Every morning, local fishermen called butanding handlers draw these gentle giants to the surface by feeding them tiny shrimp, and visitors snorkel or dive alongside them in the clear shallow water. The whale sharks here, some reaching 12 meters in length, are remarkably calm in the presence of people. Seeing one glide beneath you — enormous, slow, and utterly unhurried — is one of the most awe-inspiring things a person can experience in the natural world.

Kawasan Falls Canyoneering

On the same southern day trip from Cebu City, the town of Badian offers one of the Philippines' most thrilling adventure experiences: Kawasan Falls Canyoneering. The journey involves jumping off cliffs, swimming through narrow gorges, and sliding down natural rock chutes through the Matutinao River canyon, culminating at the stunning three-tiered Kawasan Falls — a towering cascade of milky turquoise water fed by cold mountain springs. The color of the water is almost impossibly blue, and swimming in the pool beneath the falls is a perfect end to an adrenaline-filled morning.

Moalboal & the Sardine Run

The diving town of Moalboal on Cebu's southwest coast is home to one of the ocean's great natural spectacles: a permanent school of millions of sardines that moves as one vast shimmering organism through the water just meters from the beach. Unlike seasonal sardine runs elsewhere in the world, Moalboal's sardines are here year-round, making it one of the most accessible and reliable marine wildlife experiences in Southeast Asia. The same waters hide pristine coral walls, sea turtles, and the wreck of a small fishing vessel home to a thriving artificial reef.

Cebu Lechon — A National Obsession

No visit to Cebu is complete without eating the lechon. Cebu lechon — whole roasted pig stuffed with lemongrass, garlic, onions, and local spices — is widely regarded as the finest in the Philippines, with a crackling skin so crisp it shatters at the touch and meat so flavorful it needs no sauce. The late Anthony Bourdain famously called it "the best pig, ever." Local institutions like CNT Lechon and Rico's Lechon serve it by the kilo, and lines form before they open. Budget for at least one proper lechon meal — you will not regret it.

Practical Tips

Cebu City is the ideal base, with excellent hotel options at every price point. Day trips to Oslob and Kawasan Falls can be combined (start at 4am to arrive early before crowds). Moalboal is best explored on a separate overnight trip. The Mactan-Cebu International Airport has direct flights from many Asian cities, making Cebu an easy first stop on a Philippines itinerary.
