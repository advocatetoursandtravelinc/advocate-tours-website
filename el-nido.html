The Most Beautiful Place in the Philippines

There is no shortage of superlatives for El Nido — and almost all of them are deserved. Repeatedly voted the world's best island destination by major travel publications, this small fishing town on the northern tip of Palawan sits at the edge of the Bacuit Archipelago, a collection of 45 limestone karst islands and islets rising dramatically from one of the most biodiverse marine environments in the world. The combination of dramatic geology, crystal-clear water, hidden lagoons accessible only through narrow rock passages, and beaches that appear on no map makes El Nido one of those rare destinations that travel photography cannot fully capture and words struggle to contain.

The Big Lagoon & Small Lagoon

The two lagoons of Miniloc Island are El Nido's most iconic attractions and among the most photographed natural sites in Southeast Asia. The Big Lagoon is entered through a narrow passage between two cliff faces, and the moment you emerge inside, the scale of what surrounds you is overwhelming: towering limestone walls draped in jungle vegetation, rising 200 meters above water so clear you can see every detail of the coral floor 10 meters below. You paddle through by kayak, the water shifting from pale jade near the entrance to deep emerald in the center, with nothing above but sky and circling frigatebirds.

The Small Lagoon is even more intimate — accessed by swimming or kayaking through a crack in the cliff barely wide enough to fit a kayak. Inside, you enter a completely enclosed world of vertical rock and still water, where the only sounds are your own breathing and the occasional drip from the cave ceiling above the entrance. Both lagoons can become crowded during peak hours; arriving early in the morning or late afternoon transforms the experience.

Secret Beach & Hidden Beach

Secret Beach on Matinloc Island is reached by swimming through an underwater opening in a limestone cliff — a slightly disorienting but entirely safe passage that opens into a private beach enclosed on all sides by rock. The beach is only large enough for a handful of people, and at high tide the sand almost disappears entirely, leaving only the cliff walls and the water. It is the closest thing to discovering a place that exists only for you.

Hidden Beach, also on Matinloc, is similarly dramatic — a crescent of white sand nestled between steep cliffs, accessible only from the sea, invisible from any boat until you are almost upon it. Both beaches are standard stops on the popular Tour C island-hopping route.

The Four Island-Hopping Tours

El Nido's island-hopping tours are organized into four routes — A, B, C, and D — each visiting a different set of islands, beaches, lagoons, and snorkeling spots within the Bacuit Archipelago. Tour A covers the most famous highlights including the Big Lagoon and Small Lagoon. Tour B visits the cave and the Cathedral Cave's dramatic interior. Tour C goes to Matinloc Island and its secret and hidden beaches. Tour D explores the less-visited northern islands with excellent snorkeling. Doing all four tours over four days gives a comprehensive picture of El Nido's extraordinary variety.

Nacpan Beach

About 45 minutes north of El Nido Town by habal-habal (motorbike taxi), Nacpan Beach is a four-kilometer stretch of undeveloped golden sand that represents El Nido at its most elemental: no jet skis, no beach vendors, no resort infrastructure — just a long pale curve of sand, a line of coconut palms, and the sea. At the northern end, Nacpan merges with the adjacent Calitang Beach to form a twin beach visible from the hillside above, one of the most beautiful aerial views in all of Palawan. Nacpan is the perfect antidote to the busy island-hopping routes and should not be missed.

Marine Life & Diving

El Nido sits within the Palawan Biosphere Reserve and its waters are among the most biodiverse in the Coral Triangle. Dive sites around the Bacuit Archipelago include dramatic wall dives where the reef drops from 3 meters to over 40, coral gardens of extraordinary color and density, and regular sightings of sea turtles, reef sharks, eagle rays, and Napoleon wrasse. The Dilumacad Island (Helicopter Island) dive site is a favorite for its combination of shallow coral garden and deep wall, all in a single dive.

Practical Tips

El Nido can be reached by a 5-hour scenic van ride from Puerto Princesa, or by a short direct flight from Manila on small aircraft. The town has grown significantly and now offers excellent accommodation at all price points, from dorm rooms to boutique resorts. Book accommodation in advance for December–January and Easter week. Hire a private boat for island-hopping tours if you want to control your timing and avoid the biggest crowds. The best light for photography is early morning, before the main tour groups arrive at the lagoons.
