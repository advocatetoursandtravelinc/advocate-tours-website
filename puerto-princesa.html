Palawan's Capital City

Puerto Princesa is the entry point to Palawan — the long, narrow island province that stretches like a spine from Mindoro in the north to Borneo in the south, and which has been called the Philippines' last ecological frontier. As a city, Puerto Princesa is refreshingly clean and organized by Philippine standards, known for its strict environmental ordinances and a civic pride that keeps its streets tidy and its forests intact. But visitors don't come here for the city — they come for what lies in the jungle just outside it, and in the turquoise waters of Honda Bay.

The Puerto Princesa Underground River

The Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park is the island's most celebrated attraction and one of the most remarkable natural sites in Asia. In 2012 it was voted one of the New Seven Wonders of Nature, joining a list that includes the Amazon River, Halong Bay, and Iguazu Falls. It is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site — dual recognition that reflects its global significance.

The river flows for 8.2 kilometers entirely underground through a vast cathedral-like cave system before emptying directly into the sea — making it one of the world's longest navigable underground rivers. Visitors board small wooden boats and paddle into the cave's mouth, where darkness closes in and the guides illuminate the walls with flashlights to reveal formations of stalactites and stalagmites that have been growing for millions of years. Some chambers open to heights of 60 meters, their domed ceilings encrusted with crystal. The sound of thousands of swiftlets and bats echoing in the darkness, the smell of the earth, and the sight of the river disappearing ahead into blackness create an atmosphere unlike anything else in the Philippines.

Advance permits are required and visitor numbers are strictly controlled — book well ahead, especially during peak season.

Honda Bay Island Hopping

Just north of the city, Honda Bay is a sheltered bay dotted with small islands and sandbars, each offering a slightly different experience of Palawan's legendary marine environment. The classic Honda Bay island-hopping tour visits three or four islands in a single day by bangka outrigger boat. Luli Island is named for its sandbar that "lulubog-lilitaw" (sinks and resurfaces) with the tide, creating a magical strip of white sand in the middle of the sea. Starfish Island is named for the dozens of large starfish visible in its clear shallow water. Pandan Island and Cowrie Island offer snorkeling over healthy coral gardens and simple beachside lunches of freshly grilled fish.

Ugong Rock & Eco-Adventures

Ugong Rock, about an hour from the city in Barangay Tagabinet, is a massive limestone formation rising dramatically from the forest floor. Visitors climb through a series of cave passages — squeezing through tight gaps, hauling themselves up rope ladders, and emerging through a series of natural chimneys — before reaching the summit, where a zipline carries the brave back down through the jungle canopy. The whole experience is run by the local community and proceeds go directly to the families of Tagabinet. It's physical, adventurous, and one of the most fun two hours you can have in Palawan.

Palawan Wildlife & Nature

The Palawan Wildlife Rescue and Conservation Center (formerly the Crocodile Farm) is home to rescued Philippine crocodiles, bearcat (binturong), and the remarkable Palawan pangolin — one of the world's most trafficked animals and now critically endangered. The center is both a rescue facility and an education hub, offering visitors an introduction to Palawan's extraordinary endemic wildlife. The Iwahig River Firefly Watching Tour, taken by boat after dark, is another unforgettable experience — gliding silently through mangroves lit by thousands of synchronously blinking fireflies in the trees above, like a living constellation at eye level.

Practical Tips

Puerto Princesa has a good airport with regular flights from Manila (roughly 1.5 hours). The Underground River requires a permit booked in advance — your travel agent can arrange this. Honda Bay tours depart early morning and return by early afternoon. Allow two to three days in Puerto Princesa before continuing north to El Nido or Coron by van or light aircraft. The city has a range of accommodations from budget guesthouses to comfortable mid-range hotels.
